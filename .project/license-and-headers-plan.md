# Plan: License + Copyright Headers

## Status

v1.0. One commit (mechanical edits across many files).

## Goal

Make the licensing of CodeCome legally explicit and tooling-friendly:

- root project under dual GPL-3.0-or-later / AGPL-3.0-or-later,
- `templates/sandboxes/<id>/` subtree under MIT (because those files
  get copied into user workspaces and must not contaminate them),
- short SPDX headers on every authored source file,
- `## License` sections in user-facing Markdown,
- `NOTICE` documenting the split,
- `CONTRIBUTING.md` with a Contributor License Agreement so the
  project owner retains the right to relicense or dual-license
  commercially in the future.

## Decisions (locked from the user)

| # | Decision |
|---|---|
| 1 | Dual license: `GPL-3.0-or-later OR AGPL-3.0-or-later`. SPDX expression `GPL-3.0-or-later OR AGPL-3.0-or-later`. |
| 2 | `templates/sandboxes/<id>/` is MIT. Everything else is the dual license. Other `templates/*` (e.g. `templates/finding.md`) follow the project license. |
| 3 | Header style: short SPDX + copyright. Two lines per file. Full text only in `LICENSE`, `NOTICE`, and a `## License` section in `README.md`. |
| 4 | Year span: `2025-2026`. |
| 5 | Author: `Pablo Ruiz García <pablo.ruiz@gmail.com>`. |
| 6 | Markdown files: `## License` section at the bottom of user-facing docs. No HTML-comment SPDX lines. |
| 7 | Add a `NOTICE` file at the repo root documenting both subtrees and the dual license. |
| 8 | Add `CONTRIBUTING.md` with a Contributor License Agreement statement. |

## CLA wording (in `CONTRIBUTING.md`)

Keep it short. Reasonable starting text:

    By submitting a contribution to this project, you agree that:
    1. Your contribution is licensed under the project's terms
       (GPL-3.0-or-later OR AGPL-3.0-or-later for the main project,
       MIT for files under templates/sandboxes/).
    2. You grant Pablo Ruiz García a perpetual, irrevocable,
       worldwide, royalty-free license to use, modify, sublicense
       and relicense your contribution under any terms, including
       commercial dual licensing.
    3. You have the right to make the contribution under these
       terms (you wrote it, or you have permission from the rights
       holder).

This is intentionally simple. If we want a fuller treatment later
we can adopt the Apache ICLA or Project Harmony, but those require
a separate signing flow.
